Paying players

  • To pay a player you can choose any shown player then click on that player.

  • The chosen player will get highlighted also the three blue buttons in the middle of the dash board will change into arrow buttons.

 

  • Click on the top arrow button to pay the first bet of the chosen player.

  • The calculator buttons now are active and ready to work 

  • Using these buttons just as a calculator enter the payoff of the First  bet of the selected player (the active player).

  • If the player lose on that bet then enter 0 if it's a world  Bet and roll is 7 then it's a push also enter 0 when finished press enter on the keyboard or done on the dashboard.

  • When done  an immediate response will appear in the response screen showing you the correctness of your payoff.

  • Your score will be incremented. if correct you'll get 8 points out of 20. you need to finish with all the player's payoffs to get the full 20 points.

  • Click on the middle arrow button to start paying the same player's second bet.

  • Use the same steps mentioned above to enter the payoff of the player's second bet.

  • when finished an immediate response will show you the correctness of your answer.

  • Your score will be incremented by another 8 points if your answer is  correct.

 

  • Click on the bottom arrow button to enter the player's total payoff.

  • Use the same procedure to enter the total payoff.

  • Note that you can enter the total payoff of the player right away without having to enter each bet payoff.

  • in this case if correct you'll get a bonus of 5 points

 

  • Total payoff very important note

  • If both bets win then put the total win. Or if both bets lose put 0 but:

    • If one bet win and the other  lose then there are two possibilities and that's when it gets tricky:

     A:-The wining amount of either bets is more than the other bet amount Example :

         First bet is $5 Horn Hi Yo - second bet is $9  three way craps (3 each)   - the roll is  Yo          eleven

     ___Click on the  top arrow button then put the first bet pay off which in this case is    $ 27.

    ___ Click on the  middle arrow button then put the second pay off which is lost but we care about how much we are going to give the customer for that bet which is nothing. So we put 0

    ___ Click on the bottom arrow button but wait; To get the total, it’s NOT 27 - 0=27 because we always assume that the customer likes to stay on his lost bets.

    (in this case the $9 three way craps ) So we will have to subtract that from the winning amount therefore the customer will get 27 - 9 = $18  and that's what we will put in the total because this game is about dealing with  the customer.

    B:- the wining amount of the won bet is smaller than the amount of the lost bet Example:

    First bet is $5 horn high aces - second bet is $15 Hi Lo Yo( $5 each)  - the roll is  Three craps (Acedeuce)

    ___ Click the top arrow button then put the player's first bet payoff which in this case is $11

    ___ Click the Middle arrow button then  put the second pay off which is lost but we care about how much are we going to give the customer for that particular bet which is nothing. So we put  0.

    ___ To get the total, it’s NOT 11- 0 = 11 because the customer likes to stay on his lost bets too (the $15 Hi Lo Yo ) So we will have to subtract that from the winning amount and then ask the customer for the difference which will be  11- 15 = -4   and that's what we will put in the answer to the total ( a minus sign and the difference)

     To translate that into words we will be saying " Sir or Madam, You need 4 dollars to stay up on all your bets) because this game is about dealing with  the customer.  

  • Once done with the player you are ready to start the same process with another player.

  • when done with all the players you are ready for a new roll.

 

 

  • To summarize the above and avoid the headache:

    • Paying the total, when one bet lose and the other win or a push (as in the world bet when seven rolls)

      •  put in the total field the difference between  the winning amount and the lost bet amount if the customer end up owing money to stay on his bets as they are put a minus sign then how much the customer owe.
